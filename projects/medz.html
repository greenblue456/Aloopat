Creating a Landing Page

While Landing pages are so important, not every business use them. Landing page if done right is great opportunity to create conversions and build customer base.

However its effectiveness hugely relies on the design decisions and there is a misconception that they are hard to create and maintain. Therefore, I wanted to test it out myself to see it it really was true.

What makes a successful landing page?

Before I started designing I researched and analyzed several good landing pages that were featured on muzli, unbounce and hubspot.

Common patterns among all the landing page were:

  • Clear conversion goal (e.g. getting a new user)
  • Every component in this page contributed to support this goal.
  • CTA (call-to-action) button stands out throughout the page
  • Social proof/Testimonials or customer stories
  • Answers to How/Why/ What - are you offering that solves my pain point or satisfies my desire?
  • Easily readable and few and clear links to reduce distractions.

Creating a Landing Page

Design Thinking

Empathize

For a while now I have heard many people complaining about facing challenges to get a doctors appointment when they need. Its hard and sometimes impossible to find a good doctor especially if you are new to a place and more challenging if you can’t speak local language. I recently faced similar situation where I was sick and next available appointment was 2 months later. Leading me to think how great it would be to have an app to find quick alternative doctor.

Therefore, I decided to create (landing page for) a fictional medical app Medz which could help in solving these challenges to find right doctor

Define

As I already had few experience on this issue and also heard it from almost everyone around me, I did not feel the need to create a specific person for this project.

Ideate and Prototype

With all the collected information, I drew few versions of paper wireframes to capture my ideas. I tried to combine all the important elements from What makes a successful landing page?

High Fidelity Wireframe

For landing page of Medz I decided to use currently trending illustration instead of photos to make the images easier to understand because it can break the rules of representation like that. “How it works” is often clearer when very abstracted as they need to show specific information.

As for the colours I wanted to use neutral, calm and relaxing colourtone. At the same time it had to be dependable, conservative, and flexible to represent Medz. Therefore beige with blue as secondary colour was selected.

First impression gets straight to the point with minimal distractions and clear CTA buttons including the app download options.


In case the user is not convinced right away and need more information before using the CTA button, there is a permanent ‘Get started’ CTA button visible in the fixed positioned navigation bar. This way a CTA option is always visible during scrolling and discovering the content of the landing page.

Pain points are illustrated followed by the solutions Medz offers

Steps to show that using the app is easy with just 3 simple steps.

Testimonials from other users along with their social media account to build up trust and represent real people. Once the users are familiar with the brand and its services.

One final clear CTA section at the end of page provides another opportunity for conversion.

Findings and Takeaways.

Designing a landing page was an excellent opportunity to think more deeply into this topic and experiment with different decisions that go into critical details as the placement of the CTA buttons. Although it is just a landing page, the strategy behind the creation is much more complicated than I’ve imagined. From page layout to copywriting to visual hierarchy, it really is a combination of UX and marketing.

At this point, the landing page is far from complete, since, in case of a real project, this would be the starting point to develop and launch the page to measure the reactions and interactions of the users. Next steps would be then to use the numbers to further improve and optimize the design.
