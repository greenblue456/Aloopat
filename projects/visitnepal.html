Visit Nepal

A UX case study

Travelling to Nepal? Here is everything you need for perfect trip.

Concept Background

This year, Nepal has launched a tourism campaign ‘Visit Nepal Year 2020’ with a theme of “Lifetime Experiences”. The government plans to welcome 2 million international tourists by the end of 2020. Nepal has also been named a ‘top destination for travellers to visit in 2020 and beyond’ by Forbes magazine->link
I was thrilled to find out about the launch of 2020 campaign and believe it is a perfect opportunity to get more tourists and to promote local businesses. But with quick research, I was disappointed with the lack of promotion and information available in internet about the campaign and overall tourism in Nepal.
I saw an opportunity here to develop a travel app which provides travellers with all the information they require for a perfect trip to Nepal and help make 2020 campaign successful. At the same time it could also help locals to connect and promote their services with the tourists.
I worked on this concept project from start to end that is from design to prototype. Below is the case study on how I listened to users problems and designed the app.

Challenge

How to successfully design an application platform where Tourists can find all the information’s to plan a perfect trip to Nepal?

How to promote local business?

Design Process

This will be mobile first design app as there are only few outdated and mundane apps about Nepal with very limited offerings. Design inspiration of this app came from wanting to create an app for tourists and locals which was professional and inviting. With VISIT NEPAL focus was to create a clean, trustworthy and user friendly application. Rather than creating an app that feels monotonous, I have regarded the best way to navigate and filter content on mobile devices by conducting end-to-end research and design process, from surveying both Nepalese people and non-Nepalese people to validating design decisions by usability testing with target users. My design evolved through several rounds of iteration at different stages.

Research

Research began with competitor analysis followed by interviews with 10 participants and then creating affinity mapping to derive feedbacks and deep insights on potential features, opportunities, user’s behaviours and pain points. This step provided me with the strong foundation for my app.

Competitor Analysis

I analyzed multiple existing apps about Nepal like Nepal, NepalTravel, NepalOffline and other travel apps like tripadvisor, skyscanner, Guides by lonely planet and culture trip to gather inspirations. Through this analysis I derived that the existing apps about Nepal were:

  • Outdated and not user friendly
  • Information overload
  • Missing local insights

Interviews

In order to get to know better understanding about the user opinions, motivations, pain points and needs, i conducted interview with 10 participants. This step is perfect for the exploration stage providing me with the first-hand information about users.

Questions were designed to derive following informations-

  • Concerns, fears and expectations.
  • Process, Tools and channels used to plan the trip.
  • What our users want to do
  • Possible solutions

Affinity Mapping

After the interview, I created affinity mapping to organize my results and realized the need for a solution to make planning easier. I learned that travelers had more concern before the trip, over how they planned their trip than they did during or after their trips.

User Personas

I created multiple traveler’s personas based on the initial research. The personas represent users’ goals and pain points which helped me prioritize different user needs to guide the converging phase of my design process.

Job Stories:

Keeping my personas in mind I framed job stories to explore different contexts in which a user would use the app. And to understand their motivation and desired outcome (focusing on the triggering event or situation, the motivation and goal, and the intended outcome):
When ____________(Situation)
I want to_________(Motivation)
So I can__________(Outcome)
  • When I plan my holiday, I want to easy way to find all the informations I require, So I can save endless hours required for planning.
  • When I travel to Nepal, I want to know all the basic informations beforehand, So I can enjoy my trip without stress.
  • When I travel to Nepal, I want to know about the local favourite , So I can avoid the tourist traps.

User Journey Maps

To create a good design for users it is important to think like a user. With this Customer Journey Map, I was able to understand the goals, pain points and opportunities to develop solutions for users of Visit Nepal.
Instead of creating individual user journeys, I created one map that mapped out the different stages user will be encountering in the app while trying to achieve their goal.

The “How Might We” Note Taking Method

The phrase “How Might We” was used to write the possible solutions by taking down the major and common pain points faced by most of the users.

HMW help user plan perfect trip to Nepal?
solution: By creating a complete and engaging app for users that can be used before, during and after the trip. The app will allow users to research, plan and book everything including adding pictures, updating and sharing itinerary and pictures.

HMW keep all the information clear and simple?
solution: By creating a clear sitemap, user flow and conducting users tests we could refine this step.

HMW help users find reliable guides or meet locals?
solution: By allowing local guides to create their profile and write reviews. Also listing recommendations and places to meet locals. Connect other blogger and their experiences.

Develop Phase

User Flows

User flow is created to avoid the common mistake of focusing too much on individual pages and interactions within an app. Taking a step back and concentrating on user flows to plan the trip rather than individual pages allowed me to make my design more user-centered, therefore more successful from users’ point of view. And dive into the process user would take while using search, plan and saved features to plan their trip.

Information Architecture and Navigation

Before jumping to the screens design, I created site map for the basic first layer of the app to focus on the page hierarchy.

Wireframes

Now that I had my research data and basic of information architecture ste up, next step was to create wireframes. I started with a old paper and pencil wireframes to layout the first initial ideas.

Mid Fidelity Wireframe

Next step was to refine the design once more using mid fidelity wireframes. I created wireframes for the main features of the app.

High fidelity Wireframes

Once high fidelity wireframes were ready I tested it out with 5 testers to find the issues with the design. Most of the testers gave me positive feedback with few small issues. I attended to some of the immediate problems which are highlighted below with yellow dots.

Users mentioned that discover Nepal part was quite special and attractive and wanted it to be more visible.

None of the users noticed or used tab bar. When I pointed it out they mentioned to have not noticed it at all. I also removed Maps from tab bar to Plan icon where users can view recommendations customised to their search, create or choose form available itinerary and also view any bookings they have.

In 2nd picture attractions button displayed top popular tours instead of top spots.
In booking page I added more options to choose language, add people of various age group and status and changed notes to Request to make it clear that users can write their special request to tour company.

I also changed the options bar into boxes To give the app consistent flow and to make it easier for user to click on the options.
One of the main feature I added after testing was to connect with the local and enjoy experiences with them. Users can choose from experience they want and also view the profile before booking the local.

The app is far from complete and needs more tests and updates which I will continue to work on.
